The architecture of colony-building insects is an extension of their own physiology. The impressive structures created by honeybees function like internal organs. Honeycomb is purposefully engineered to support social behaviors and assist the colony in regulating temperature, humidity, and carbon dioxide. Commissioned by the University of Colorado Boulder, Justin Bellucci and Kimberly Drennan set out to showcase this unique architecture though a digital fabrication installation temporarily displayed in the SEEC building on east campus.

Parametric design. Infinite possibilities.

Using Grasshopper, a parametric model was built to explore various geometries, tolerances, and material types without having to build a separate 3D model for each iteration.

Form-finding in Rhino3D. 220 unique pieces.

An exact 3D model of the pavilion was generated using Rhino3D and Grasshopper. Once the final design was agreed upon, the model was exported to the CNC machine software for fabrication.

CNC Fabrication. Lots of sanding.

Using a 3-axis CNC machine, each piece was precisely milled from 3/4" plywood. All 440 pieces were sanded, labeled, and finished to ensure a beautiful final product.

Construction. No glue!

File to factory fabrication allows the team to construct the pavilion in one day with no fasteners or glue.
